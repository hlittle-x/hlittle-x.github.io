it's strange how things happen, sometimes. my site has never done more traffic than it is doing right now, and yet, i'm shying away from it. i've made many friends and sustained even more enemies, and have been pinged by almost every country in the world, and yet - i don't really feel it, anymore. i feel it is mastrubation of a sort - and while mastrubation is a good thing, it rarely is productive for anything but the most immediate sort of relief. i've felt things slipping backwards over time without rewriting the past, and it is towards myself that i have been drawing, more and more, closer and closer. my greatest productivity comes from the moments where i am most alone - and even the surrogate life that the internet provides me, that life of the Un-not ReaL, is doing nothing but keeping my time in circles, using what talents I have for reasons not for the greatest - and that is to say, the most desired good - and i need them all. i think of life as something each person comes to master, or those that are lucky enough to grow into what it is they want to be or who they wish to become - and lately, my self-aggrandizing moments over the internet do little to encourage me on the path i feel i must take. considering everything i want hinges on my ability to function as an author independent of the interweb (as i've no intention to ever publish seriously upon it - i believe too strongly in the written word) whether it be graduate schools to gain acceptance to, or an additive income to salt away, or just the ability to wake up late mornings and work from home with my cats puddled around my feet like licorice on a hot day...and all known writers talk of the distractions of the everyday, whether it be telephone calls or videocassette machines, television - that bulbous glass teat of disconcentration - or even company, but never to the extremes of thoreau's "walden". i suppose the internet is too distracting for me, and it is disconnecting me from what i need to be doing, while reconnecting me with a thousand different lives, ideas, faces, wonders, sweet daydreams are lost, are bought and sold. these are shortcuts to thinking, for now. i need all of my thoughts, each and every, even only.

this past few weeks have been interesting. spent time with Kipp. retreated to the Shortest Penninsula for letters delivered past dusk. listened to much music. failed to get tickets to see the yeah yeah yeahs. bought a kazillion books from numerous corner vendors. i'm especially enjoying this fifties book on etiquette. it cost a dollar, hardcover, brand new in 1956. and it cost me the same thing, in wonderful condition, in 2002. have gone on many walks. talking on the telephone more. enjoying living alone. have covered myself in many cats. am writing letters again by hand, slower now, but surer still. sleeping full nights. watching movies and listening to the foghorns, the fire engines, the sounds of the street and the absence of birds in the morning. i want to wake up to the sounds of birds again. the songs of the silent dawn. it's amazing how the crease of a binding beneath your fingertips, or looking at letters penned under museum glass, can bring such clarity home. the airconditioner paints white noise epitaphs beneath my eyelids, and i sleep freely. i wish there was more time in the morning. i'd like to watch the films again. i'd like to walk from corner to corner, learning the sidewalk squares, skipping in hopscotch steps when no one chanced to look, and even then, maybe. but for now, it's time to look at writing as what it is - a responsibility, not a birthright. and honesty. yes, and honesty too.
